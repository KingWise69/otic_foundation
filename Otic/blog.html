AI in Uganda: Challenges and Opportunities

Artificial intelligence (AI) refers to a wide range of technologies that allow machines to perform tasks that normally require human intelligence, such as learning, reasoning, problem solving, and decision making. Today, AI is widely integrated in various fields, including healthcare, finance, agriculture, and education, transforming the human lifestyle and workplaces.

Globally, developed nations are strong proponents of this technology. However, as AI continues to revolutionize various sectors worldwide, countries like Uganda are at a crossroads, grappling with both the promises and perils of this transformative technology.

Current Statistics and Public Perception

Recent statistics paint a complex picture of AI's impact and perception in the country, underscoring the need for strategic interventions to harness its potential while addressing pressing challenges.
(i).57% of Ugandans believe AI will mostly harm the country.
(ii).70% of Ugandans are afraid that their personal data would be exploited without their permission (Lloyds Register Foundation survey).

This high level of distrust can be attributed to factors such as limited internet access, lack of AI literacy, and concerns over data privacy and security.

Data Protection and Privacy Regulations

As AI systems often rely on large amounts of personal data to function effectively, it is crucial for Uganda to develop robust data protection and privacy regulations focused on the use and development of AI tools in the country. This policy should prioritize:
(i).Transparency
(ii).Accountability
(iii).User control over personal data

These measures are essential to allay public fears about artificial intelligence (AI).

Raising Awareness and Addressing Challenges

In addition to this, the Ugandan government and stakeholders must prioritize raising awareness about AI's benefits and ethical considerations, while also addressing:
(i).Infrastructure limitations
(ii).Skill gaps
(iii).Comprehensive data protection policies

Digital Divide in Uganda

The digital divide in Uganda is another pressing issue highlighted by the statistics. According to the Uganda Bureau of Statistics (UBOS), the digital divide in the country is still very big because rural and urban communities do not have reliable access to affordable electricity, which limits access to the internet. This striking difference emphasizes the inaccessibility of AI-powered solutions in most communities.

The government and stakeholders must prioritize expanding internet access in rural and underserved areas through initiatives like the National Broadband Policy .

Opportunities for Sustainable Development

Despite these challenges, AI also presents immense opportunities for Uganda's sustainable development. The statistics reveal that AI has the potential to contribute to 13 out of 17 UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) in the country.

Integrating artificial intelligence across various sectors in Uganda, from healthcare to agriculture, will help make notable strides in achieving development goals.
